THE 9.45 FROM OWLPEN HALT

Characters

The Stranger ( Quite elderly)

Alan

Betty (Alan's wife)

Tom (Also elderly)

Jennifer (A young woman)

The scene is a draughty old railway waiting room at a small English village.

The next train is due at 21.45. The stranger sits alone, quietly humming to himself.

The Stranger: hmmmm mmmmm hmmmm hmmm

(After a few moments a creaky door opens and Alan and Betty enter the waiting room)

Alan: Brrrr it is cold out there tonight

Betty: Yes I am chilled to the marrow.

Alan: Good job you wore your new warm coat.

Betty: It certainly is. Let's sit down then. We have about 10 minutes before the train arrives.

The Stranger: I think it may be delayed tonight

Alan: Oh, good evening sir, why do you say that?

The Stranger: You know, sometimes we just get a feeling about something.

Betty: Yes, that's true enough, but I hope it isn't too late

Alan: Are you travelling through to Norwich?

The Stranger: Yes. It will be Norwich for me today.

Betty: We are going there too.

(The creaky door opens once more as Tom enters)

Alan: Hello Tom, how are you?

Tom: Very cold I can say that.

Betty: Freezing out there tonight isn't it Tom?

Tom: Yes, and not much warmer in here. Too many draughts.

The Stranger: It's a very old building.

Alan: Very true, been here all my life.

Betty: It would be nice if they would build a new one.

Tom: Not much chance of that. This line will probably be closed down before too long.

Alan: Unfortunately, very true. Not many of these remote branch lines are left now.

Tom: I haven't seen you here before sir. Have you just moved to this area?

The Stranger: No, I don't live around these parts, just passing through.

Alan: Not often we see a strange face here.

Betty: Hardly ever.

( The creaky door opens once again and the wind is blowing as Jennifer enters the waiting room.)

Betty: Hello Jenny, nice to see you

Jennifer: Hello everyone. My goodness it is blowing so hard out there now and I thought I may have missed the train.

Betty; No you haven't missed it, come and sit over here out of the draught

Jennifer: Thanks Betty

The Stranger: The tain will be late today.

Tom: Well, maybe, mostly it is on time though.

The Stranger: Not tonight though.

Alan: You seem very sure of that. Do you work for the railway?

The Stranger: Ha! No, I have never worked for the rail company.

Alan: Ok, I just wondered.

Jennifer: I hope the train is warm.

Betty. So do I.

Jennifer: I had to run nearly all the way here, not easy in these heels.

Betty: You always leave things to the last minute.

Jennifer: I know, I should be better organised. Yesterday I left a note out for the milkman asking for an extra pint.

Alan: That doesn't seem disorganised.

Jennifer: It is really. You see, I don't have milk delivered

Betty: Jennifer you are so silly sometimes.

Tom: Another five minutes for the train

The Stranger: Could be longer

Alan: Perhaps.

Tom. Tell me sir, what brings you to Owlpen?

The Stranger: Just wandering through.

Tom: I see. Do you do that a lot? Wander?

The Stranger: Yes, I do it all the time

Alan: Interesting.

The Stranger: I find it so.

Jennifer: I must say I wouldn't want to wander far in these heels.

(Jennifer and Betty laugh at this)

The Stranger: I am sure I would also find that difficult.

Tom: Do you work or just wander? Excuse my inquisitiveness

The Stranger: I don't mind people being inquisitive, in fact, I rather enjoy it. And to answer your question, I do work but it is rather strange work.

Betty: Now that sounds interesting. Strange in what way?

The Stranger: Hmm, at this point of a conversation I am often tempted to say 'Between you and me' or 'Don't let this go any further' or even 'never tell this to anyone else' but really there is no need.

Alan: Now I am sure you have us all very puzzled.

The Stranger: I can understand why it may seem puzzling but in fact it is quite simple

Tom: If so simple then tell us, what do you do.

Jennifer: I think he is a spy

The Stranger: (laughs) No, no, no. Not a spy. Firstly though, let me tell you why I don't have to ask you to be secretive about it.

Tom: yes that would be a good start.

Jennifer: I am no good at keeping secrets anyway

Betty: Nor me (Jenny and Betty laugh)

Alan: I could take a secret to the grave but in this case, it doesn't seem necessary.

The Stranger: Quite. And the reason you do not need to be secretive is that if you told anyone they would not believe you anyway.

Alan: Now it is getting very curious.

The Stranger: In fact, I am almost certain that none of you will believe what I say.

Tom: I like to keep an open mind.

The Stranger: As we all should.

Alan: So are we to know what it is? This thing that you feel we will not believe.

The Stranger. Yes, yes, I am sorry for delaying it.

Tom: we still have a few minutes before the train arrives.

The Stranger: It will be late anyway

Alan: As you have said.

The Stranger: I would say I have a role rather than a job or occupation.

Jennifer: Ooooh are you an actor then?

The Stranger: Not an actor. Although to be frank, I suppose it does require a certain amount of acting.

Tom: But not on the stage?

The Stranger: No

Betty: Or in the movies?

The Stranger: No, I would rather hope I am not in the entertainment business, although sometimes I wonder?

Tom: So put us out of our misery, what is this errr.. role?

The stranger: I am king Charles

(Everyone but the stranger laughs)

Alan: Ok you had us all fooled there for a moment. I thought you were really going to tell us what you do.

Jennifer: Do you mean prince charles?

Alan: It is king Charles now Jennifer, don't you remember

Jennifer: Oh yes of course. Sorry your majesty.

Tom: Jennifer, you don't have to call him your majesty. He isn't really King Charles.

The Stranger: Ah but I am, and as I said. You don't believe me.

Alan: I hate to sound unkind but how can you possibly be King Charles. I mean, we all know what King Charles looks like and you look nothing like him

Jennifer: I have only seen him on the telly

The Stranger: I am afraid you are wrong there my friend. Until this evening you had no idea what King Charles looks like.

Tom: I beg to differ there. I am sure we all know what he looks like.

The Stranger: The man you know as King Charles is in fact not King Charles, I am. He is somebody that people are led to believe is King Charles.

Betty: Why would that be? I am always ready to give the benefit of the doubt but this really does seem a bit far fetched.

Tom: But harmless

The Stranger: Harmless?

Tom: Yes, if you want to believe you are King Charles I am sure it hurts nobody else

Jennifer: Unless the real King Charles finds out

The Stranger (With a little chuckle) But of course. I am the real one.

Betty: So what about Camilla?

The Stranger: What about her?

Betty: Well, is she the real Camilla?

The Stranger: She is real but her name is not Camilla, it is Phylis.

Alan: And the person we know as King Charles. What is his name?

The Stranger: Nigel, since 2019 anyway.

Tom: You mean he changed his name? This, Hmm, I suppose you would call an actor, changed his name?

The Stranger: No. Not at all. He is not the same person as pre 2019

Betty: Not the same person?

The Stranger. No, they change them around every few years. They get bored with the job very easily pretending to be somebody else.

Jennifer: A bit like the actors in tv soaps

The Stranger: A bit like that yes.

Alan: The train is late.

The Stranger: I thought it would be.

Alan: So what I am thinking is why? I mean, if we believed that you are King Charles. Why is there someone playing your part?

The Stranger: It all started in the summer of 1840 with the attempted assassination of Queen Victoria, my great, great, great grandmother

Jennifer: Wow! a long time ago.

The stranger: Yes indeed. After that it was decided the royal family would stay well away from the public and be impersonated by, what I suppose, are actors.

Alan; This is a great story but really hardly believable at all.

The stranger: As I told you.

Tom: I can actually believe that may happen, Many things are kept secret from the public.

Alan: Yes, that is true enough.

Tom: However sir. I am 100% totally convinced that you are not King Charles.

The Stranger: That is your prerogative of course.

Tom: But you tell the story very well.

The Stranger: I appreciate that, thank you. Maybe I tell it well because it is a true story.

Tom: And maybe not.

The Stranger: As you wish.

Jennifer: I am not sure whether to believe it or not. I am so indecisive.

Betty: Have you always been like that then Jen?

Jennifer: I am not sure.

Alan. I must say I don't believe it either but I wish you well sir and it was nice to meet you?

Tom: One more thing I would like to ask, if you wouldn't mind. If we did believe you are the real king Charles, what are you doing here?

The Stranger: Everybody has to be somewhere.

Tom: Hmmm, You did your research well.

The Stranger: My research?

Tom: Yes, that line you just spoke. It was a line from an episode of a radio show called the goons. It was one of the then Prince Charles's favourite shows.

The Stranger. Yes. It was. I loved that show and was so sad that I could not meet the cast, only my impersonator could do that.

Tom: Yes I can imagine that would have been very frustrating for the real Prince Charles.

The Stranger: Which I was of course.

Betty: Shhhh, I think I hear the train.

Jennifer: Yes I do too, it's coming, and only a few minutes late.

The Stranger: Please excuse me ladies and gentlemen. I want to make my way to the end of the platform, I prefer to sit at the back of the train. A very good evening to you all.

Jennifer: Goodbye your Majesty.

Alan: Nice to meet you sir, enjoy your onward journey

The Stranger. Thank you, I will.

Tom: a most interesting conversation my friend, more interesting than you can imagine.

The Stranger: I am pleased I kept you amused. Once again, good day to you all,

(The creaky door opens as he leaves)

Betty: Come on Jen, I will help you along the platform in those heels.

Jennifer. Aw thanks Betty, lets make a move

Tom: Bye you two.

Jennifer and Betty Together: Bye Tom

Alan: What an interesting old fellow, living in his little dream world.

Tom: He is an imposter of course but much of what he said is true?

Alan: Really? Surely not.

Tom: Oh yes, much of it in fact

Alan: I find that difficult to believe, surely it was all just fantasy, in his mind?

Tom: Not at all. As a matter of fact I know for certain that a lot of what he said was true, I just don't know how he knew about it.

Alan: Well if you know most of it was true,how do you know about it

. Tom: Quite simple really Alan. I don't mean to shock you. But actually. I am the real King Charles.

THE END
